Dominoes on chess boards

In class, we proved that it's impossible to cover a checkerboard with opposite corners removed using 2 by 1 dominoes. The reason is that all those dominoes cover exactly one black and exactly one white square, but the board we are given has a different number of black and white squares.

Call a subset S of a checkerboard balanced if it has the same number of black and white squares. Can every balanced, connected subset of a chess board be tiled by dominoes?
